Stadtentwicklung Schlieren – Dietikon

Schlieren and Dietikon are two adjacent municipalities. They are located in the Limmattal, a region in the northern agglomeration of Zurich, Switzerland. The ongoing urbanization process, perceived during the last decades in Switzerland, induces an impressive demographic change in this region. The once rural region with small, scattered villages transformed to an agglomeration landscape where the settlement area has almost merged into one linear city (Koch et al., 2003). Connectivity between the forested hill and the river, is severely disturbed and fragmented by railways, highways and wide spread settlement areas (Grün Stadt Zürich, 2006a). The fine-grained political structure with 16 municipalities belonging to two different cantons, Zurich and Aargau, complicates comprehensive analyses and integrated concept development (Koch et al., 2003). This makes enhancing ecological and social sustainability of the region a difficult task.
